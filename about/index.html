About me

Who writes this, why it exists, and what I actually do day to day.

Hi — I'm a cybersecurity practitioner working in blue team operations. I spend my days in SIEM dashboards, chasing down detections, tuning alert logic, and occasionally trying to break things in a lab so I understand how to defend against them better.

Cyber Credo is my field notebook. I write here because I couldn't find the kind of content I was looking for when I was starting out — practical, honest, tool-specific, written by someone actually doing the work rather than summarising vendor documentation.

What I do

Blue team operations: detection engineering, SIEM tuning, threat hunting, and incident triage. Most of my time is split between writing detection logic, investigating alerts, and trying to reduce the signal-to-noise ratio in our logging pipeline.

When I'm not in the SOC, I'm usually in a home lab — running attack simulations, testing new Sigma rules, or grinding through OSCP prep on Hack The Box and TryHackMe.

Earned: (ISC)² CC, CompTIA Security+. Currently studying toward OSCP. After that, GCIH is on the roadmap. I write about the cert prep process as I go — you can find those posts in the blog and the full cert guide on the certifications page.

Tools I use daily

Splunk, ELK Stack, Sigma rules, Wireshark, Nessus, Python, Bash — and a lot of grep. If I write about a tool on this blog, it's because I've used it in a real or lab environment. I don't do sponsored tool reviews or vendor-supplied content.

Why "Cyber Credo"

A credo is a statement of belief. Mine: that good security work is built on understanding, not compliance checklists. That the best defenders think like attackers. That documentation is a force multiplier. And that most useful knowledge in this field lives in people's heads and terminal histories, not in official training materials — which is exactly why I write it down here.
