About me

Who writes this, why it exists, and what you'll find here.

Hi — I'm a cybersecurity practitioner working in blue team operations. I spend my days in SIEM dashboards, chasing detections, tuning alert logic, and occasionally breaking things in a lab so I understand how to defend against them better.

Cyber Credo is where I document what I learn — practical, tool-specific, written by someone doing the work rather than summarising vendor documentation. Everything published here comes from real lab or field experience.

What's on this site

The blog is the core — written guides and walkthroughs covering detection engineering, threat hunting, incident response, SIEM configuration, and more. New posts go up regularly.

The topics page lets you browse by domain — SIEM, EDR, WAF, IR, VMDR, Threat Intelligence, Offensive Security, Malware Analysis, and Linux. Click a topic to see every guide written on it in one place.

The videos page collects my YouTube walkthroughs — lab builds, tool demos, and technique breakdowns in video format for when reading isn't the right medium.

What I do

Blue team operations: detection engineering, SIEM tuning, threat hunting, and incident triage. Most of my time is split between writing detection logic, investigating alerts, and reducing signal-to-noise in logging pipelines.

Outside the SOC, I'm usually in a home lab — running attack simulations, testing Sigma rules, or working through offensive security material on Hack The Box and TryHackMe. Understanding how attacks are built is the most useful foundation you can have for detection work.

Why "Cyber Credo"

A credo is a statement of belief. Mine: that good security work is built on understanding, not compliance checklists. That the best defenders think like attackers. That most useful knowledge in this field lives in people's heads and terminal histories — which is why I write it down here.
